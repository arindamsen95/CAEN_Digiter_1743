The linear interpolation itself is not the time resolution. It is a way to estimate the pulse crossing time more precisely than the sampling period.
Let's look at it geometrically.
Suppose your sampling period is:
dt=312.5" ps"

and your CFD threshold is:
V_thr=-5" mV"

Now imagine two consecutive samples:
Sample	Time (ns)	Voltage (mV)
i−1	10.0000	-4
i	10.3125	-8
The threshold (-5 mV) lies somewhere between these two samples.
Without interpolation, you only know:
threshold crossed between
10.0000 ns and 10.3125 ns
which gives a timing uncertainty of roughly one sample:
312.5" ps"

Linear interpolation
Assume the waveform is approximately a straight line between the two samples.
The line equation is:
V(t)=V_1+(V_2-V_1)/(t_2-t_1 )(t-t_1)

Set V(t)=V_thr:
V_thr=V_1+(V_2-V_1)/(t_2-t_1 )(t-t_1)

Solving for t:
t=t_1+(V_thr-V_1)/(V_2-V_1 )(t_2-t_1)

which is exactly
t_cross = t1 + (threshold - v1)*(t2 - t1)/(v2 - v1);

Example
Using:
t1 = 10.0000 ns
v1 = -4 mV

t2 = 10.3125 ns
v2 = -8 mV

threshold = -5 mV
Compute:
(-5-(-4))/(-8-(-4))=(-1)/(-4)=0.25

Therefore
t_cross=10.0000+0.25×0.3125=10.078125" ns"

Instead of knowing only that the crossing occurred somewhere in a 312.5 ps interval, you now estimate it at:
10.078125" ns"

Why this improves timing
Your digitizer samples every 312.5 ps, but the analog signal exists continuously between samples.
Interpolation uses the waveform shape to estimate where the threshold crossing occurred between the recorded points.
So even with a 312.5 ps sampling interval, you can often achieve timing precision much smaller than 312.5 ps.
For example, many systems with:
312.5" ps/sample"

can achieve:
50-100" ps"

timing resolution after CFD and interpolation.

Then where does the time resolution come from?
For each event:
	Extract t_1from detector 1 using interpolation. 
	Extract t_2from detector 2 using interpolation. 
	Compute 
Δt=t_1-t_2

for thousands of events.
	Plot a histogram of Δt. 
	Fit a Gaussian: 
f(Δt)=Aexp⁡(-(Δt-μ)^2/(2σ^2 ))

The fitted σis the measured timing resolution of the detector pair.
So:
	Interpolation → gives a better estimate of the time for a single event. 
	Histogram width (σ) → gives the actual time resolution over many events
